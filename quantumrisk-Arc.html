Research abstract
IEEE · Cloud Security · 2026

Modern cloud-native systems increasingly rely on microservice architectures, where applications are decomposed into loosely coupled, independently deployable services. While this architecture improves scalability and resilience, it also introduces complex interdependencies that make cyber risk assessment and prioritization significantly more challenging. In such systems, the compromise or failure of a single service can propagate through dependencies, resulting in cascading risk that cannot be accurately captured by traditional static risk scoring methods.

This research proposes a quantum-inspired optimization framework for cascading risk assessment and mitigation in microservice-based environments. The proposed approach models microservices and their dependencies as an attack graph and formulates risk minimization as a Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) problem. The framework aims to dynamically prioritize mitigation actions under resource constraints while accounting for inter-service risk propagation. Quantum-inspired solvers, such as simulated annealing and hybrid optimization techniques, are proposed to efficiently explore the large combinatorial search space inherent in such systems.

Simulated Annealing

Quantum-inspired solver

Simulated annealing traverses the QUBO energy landscape, escaping local minima via controlled temperature schedules to find near-optimal mitigation sets.

Annealing solver mechanics
Initial temperature T₀ set proportional to max(|Q_ij|) to ensure global exploration
Geometric cooling schedule: T_{k+1} = α·T_k, α = 0.995 (tunable per cluster size)
Metropolis acceptance: P(accept) = exp(-ΔE / T) for uphill moves
Hybrid mode: SA warm-start feeds D-Wave Leap for hardware acceleration
~94% optimal gap on benchmark graphs

QUBO problem formulation
Risk minimization as a QUBO problem
Formulated for n microservices with inter-service coupling matrix Q
Quantum-Inspired
# Objective: minimize total cascading risk cost
H(x) = Σᵢ Qᵢᵢ · xᵢ + Σᵢ<ⱼ Qᵢⱼ · xᵢ · xⱼ

# Subject to resource budget constraint (embedded as penalty)
H(x) += λ · (Σᵢ cᵢ · xᵢ − B)²

# where xᵢ ∈ {0,1}: 1 = mitigate service i, 0 = skip
# Qᵢᵢ = individual service risk score (CVSS × EPSS × exposure)
# Qᵢⱼ = propagation coupling: P(j compromised | i compromised)
# cᵢ = remediation cost for service i · B = total budget
xᵢ ∈ {0,1}
Binary decision variable. 1 = apply mitigation to service i
Qᵢᵢ (diagonal)
Individual risk: CVSS score × EPSS exploit probability × attack surface
Qᵢⱼ (off-diagonal)
Coupling term: risk propagation probability across service dependency edge i→j
λ (penalty)
Lagrange multiplier enforcing budget constraint B into unconstrained form
cᵢ (cost)
Engineering hours × severity weight required to remediate service i
B (budget)
Total remediation capacity available in current sprint / incident window

About the research

Research meets production security

QuantumRisk AI originated as an advanced research initiative at NED University of Engineering and Technology, Karachi. The mission: bridge theoretical quantum-inspired optimization with the practical demands of cloud-native security operations.

The engine models a live Kubernetes cluster as a stochastic attack graph, formulates risk minimization as a QUBO problem, and applies simulated annealing to produce ranked, resource-aware mitigation playbooks — all within the latency requirements of real incident response.

The prototype integrates with Amazon EKS via eBPF probes, Kubernetes admission webhooks, and live CVE/EPSS feeds — demonstrating that quantum-inspired techniques can operate at production scale.
